# Health & Lifestyle Risk Analytics Dashboard (Power BI | Data Analytics Portfolio)

Data-driven Power BI dashboard analyzing health & lifestyle data to explore relationships between stress, sleep, physical activity, and cardiovascular indicators using DAX and interactive visualization.


## Dashboard Preview
![Dashboard](Health_and_Lifestyle_Risk_Analysis_Dashboard.png)


## Project Summary

Understanding how lifestyle factors affect stress and health is important for wellness monitoring and risk awareness. This dashboard analyzes the relationship between **sleep, stress, physical activity, BMI, heart rate, and blood pressure** to uncover measurable patterns and potential health indicators using interactive visual analytics.

This project was created as a portfolio demonstration of foundational **Data Analytics and Power BI skills**.


## Business Problem

Wellness and health-focused teams often need clear, data-driven insights to understand how lifestyle behaviors impact stress, sleep quality, and cardiovascular indicators. Without proper visualization, identifying patterns and risk relationships is difficult.

This dashboard was built to explore how **physical activity, sleep, BMI, and heart rate influence stress and overall health indicators**, enabling better understanding through interactive data visualization.


## Key Statistics from the Data

- **374 individuals analyzed**
- **Average Stress Level:** 5.39 / 10  
- **Average Sleep Duration:** 7.13 hours  
- **Patient Age Range:** 27 – 59 years  
- **58.56%** reported **no sleep disorder**
- **~41%** experienced **Insomnia or Sleep Apnea**
- Higher BMI and lower physical activity are associated with **increased stress and blood pressure**


## Key Insights

- Higher **physical activity levels** are generally associated with **lower stress and healthier heart rate patterns**
- **Sleep quality decreases as stress increases**, showing a negative relationship between stress and recovery
- Higher **BMI groups tend to show elevated blood pressure**, indicating increased cardiovascular risk
- Stress varies across **occupations**, suggesting lifestyle and workload influence
- Sleep disorders (Insomnia & Sleep Apnea) impact **sleep duration and stress levels**

## Data-Driven Recommendations

Based on the analysis of 374 individuals aged 27–59, several data-backed recommendations can be derived:

- **Increase Physical Activity Programs**  
  Individuals with higher physical activity levels tend to exhibit lower stress levels and healthier average heart rate patterns. Encouraging regular physical activity may contribute to stress reduction and improved cardiovascular indicators.

- **Prioritize Sleep Quality Improvement**  
  With approximately 41% of individuals experiencing Insomnia or Sleep Apnea, and observable declines in sleep quality as stress increases, sleep-focused interventions may significantly impact stress management and overall wellness.

- **Monitor High BMI Groups for Cardiovascular Risk**  
  Higher BMI categories show elevated systolic and diastolic blood pressure averages. Regular monitoring and targeted health programs may help mitigate long-term cardiovascular risks.

- **Target Stress Management by Occupation & Age Trends**  
  Stress levels vary across occupation groups and age segments. Identifying high-stress categories can support more tailored wellness initiatives.

- **Integrate Stress & Sleep Monitoring Together**  
  Since average stress level (5.39/10) and average sleep duration (7.13 hours) show interrelated patterns, combining stress and sleep tracking can improve early risk detection.
